Description

This commanding Italian Baroque armchair showcases the period's sculptural approach to furniture design. The walnut frame features deeply carved acanthus leaves, scrolling arms terminating in volutes, and cabriole legs with ball-and-claw feet.

The chair has been reupholstered in period-appropriate Genoese velvet in deep crimson, complementing the rich patina of the carved wood. The overall effect is one of power and prestige.

History & Provenance

Historical Context

Originally created for a Roman palazzo circa 1680–1700, this armchair would have been part of a suite of seat furniture for the main reception room.

The quality of carving suggests a major workshop, possibly connected to papal commissions. The chair represents the height of Italian Baroque furniture making.

Provenance

Roman workshop, circa 1690; Italian noble family, Rome, 1690–1850; Acquired by English collector on Grand Tour, 1850; English country house collection, 1850–1975; Professional reupholstery, 2005; Private collection, London, 1975–present.
